Menu Card: How to Decode the Cipher

  • Caesar Cipher: A shift cipher where each letter is shifted by a certain number in the alphabet.
  • Shift Value: In this case, the shift is 5. This means each letter in the ciphertext is shifted by 5 positions backward in the alphabet.
  • Example: The letter 'F' would be decoded to 'A', 'G' would be decoded to 'B', and so on.
  • How to Decode: Use the shift value (5 in this case) to move each letter back by 5 positions in the alphabet.
